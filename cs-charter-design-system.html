The Challenge

Consistency at Scale
Across Every Touchpoint

As Spectrum Business scaled its digital presence — with dozens of product pages, vertical landing pages, and promotional campaigns launching per quarter — design inconsistency became a real liability. Components were being recreated from scratch across teams, icons weren't standardized, and the mobile experience diverged from desktop in ways that hurt brand trust.

I led the development and enforcement of a comprehensive Spectrum Business design system that gave every designer, developer, and AEM author a single source of truth. This meant building out the full component library, documenting patterns, and personally mentoring new hires on how to use the system correctly.

System Architecture

What the Design System Covers

🎨
Color Tokens
Named semantic tokens (primary, secondary, surface, error, success) mapped to exact hex values, ensuring consistency between XD designs, AEM components, and front-end CSS variables.
Aa
Typography Scale
A complete type hierarchy from display headings down to captions — with responsive sizing specs, line-height standards, and weight rules for every context.
⊞
Component Library
200+ reusable components covering hero modules, pricing tables, CTAs, navigation patterns, cards, icons, and promotional banners — each with desktop, tablet, and mobile variants.
📐
Grid & Spacing
Standardized 12-column grid with defined breakpoints and consistent spacing scale (4px base unit) used across every page template in AEM.

Mentorship & Adoption

Building Team Capability
Beyond the System

A design system is only as good as its adoption. I mentored new hires on using Adobe Experience Manager, navigating the design system components, and writing light HTML/CSS — accelerating onboarding time and ensuring the system was actually used rather than ignored. Sprint velocity increased measurably as the team became fluent in the shared component vocabulary.
