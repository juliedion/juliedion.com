The Challenge

Converting SMB Decision-Makers
at Scale

Spectrum Business needed to evolve its small-business homepage to more effectively communicate bundled plan value — Internet, Phone, Mobile, and TV — to business owners making quick, high-stakes decisions. The existing page lacked a clear hierarchy, failed on mobile, and wasn't performing against aggressive promotional deadlines.

Working as Senior UI Designer embedded on the ITG contractor team within Charter Communications, I led design across all breakpoints and then authored the final pages directly in Adobe Experience Manager (AEM) for production deployment.

My Process

From Wireframes to Live Production

Each sprint moved through a disciplined process — from stakeholder briefing to XD prototypes, cross-functional review, and final AEM authoring. Design decisions were validated against user research and A/B testing data.

01
Discovery & Briefing
Aligned with product, marketing, and engineering stakeholders to define promotional goals, user segments (SMB owners, office managers), and content priorities for each sprint.
02
Wireframes in Adobe XD
Rapidly produced low-to-mid fidelity wireframes across three breakpoints (desktop, tablet, mobile) to validate layout, hierarchy, and CTA placement before full design.
03

Applied the Spectrum Business design system — standardized icons, typography, color tokens, and component patterns — to produce pixel-perfect prototypes for stakeholder sign-off.

AEM Authoring & Launch
Authored the final designs directly in Adobe Experience Manager, working alongside front-end developers to QA across browsers and devices before each promotional launch.

Collaboration

How a Page Gets Made
at Charter Spectrum

Every page update followed a structured cross-functional workflow — from a Jira ticket to a live URL — with handoffs between creative leadership, content, SEO, design, development, and QA.

Creative Manager
Jira Ticket Created

The creative manager opened a Jira ticket scoped to the sprint — defining the promotional objective (e.g., "Stack your savings" bundle push), target audience, page location, and deadline. This ticket anchored all downstream work and tracked status through every handoff.

Copywriters
Messaging & Copy Development

Copywriters drafted headline options, body copy, and CTA language aligned to the promotional offer. Multiple rounds of copy were written to hit tone, word count limits, and the SMB audience's decision-making language — focused on value, speed, and reliability.

SEO Team
SEO Review & Keyword Alignment

Copy was routed to the SEO team before any design began. They reviewed headline structure, keyword density, meta descriptions, and H-tag hierarchy to ensure the page would perform in search. Edits came back marked up in the Jira ticket with required changes flagged as blocking.

Design Team — Julie Dion, Senior UI Designer
Mockups in Adobe XD & Figma

With approved copy in hand, I built high-fidelity mockups across all three breakpoints — desktop, tablet, and mobile — in Adobe XD using the Spectrum Business design system. Component tokens, spacing rules, and color variables kept designs consistent across the 200+ component library. Figma was used for collaborative annotation and stakeholder review presentations.

Adobe XD Figma Spectrum Design System
Development & AEM Authoring
Prototypes Built in Adobe Experience Manager

Approved designs were handed off to the development team and — uniquely — I also authored pages directly in AEM myself. Working inside Charter's production CMS, I placed components, set tokens, configured promotional modules, and previewed across breakpoints before passing to front-end developers for final QA. This dual role significantly reduced revision cycles and gave me direct control over the live output.

Adobe Experience Manager Dev Handoff
Live
Page Launched on spectrum.com/business

After cross-browser QA, stakeholder sign-off, and a final promotional deadline review, the page went live on Spectrum Business — reaching 45M+ monthly visitors. Post-launch, performance data was fed back into the next sprint's Jira ticket to iterate on what moved the needle.
