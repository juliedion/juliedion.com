The Challenge

Selling Complexity
Clearly

Spectrum Business's five core product pages — Internet, Phone, TV, Mobile, and Bundles — are the highest-traffic pages in the SMB funnel. Each product has multiple tiers, promotional pricing, and technical differentiators that time-pressed business owners need to evaluate in seconds.

The existing pages buried premium positioning, made plan comparison difficult on mobile, and didn't leverage cross-sell opportunities between products. I redesigned all five pages from the ground up — applying consistent design system patterns while giving each product its own clear hierarchy, visual differentiation, and AEM-authored module structure.

Process

How I Approached It

01
Competitive Audit
Reviewed how AT&T Business, Comcast Business, and Verizon Business structured their product pages — identifying best practices in plan comparison UX and promotional hierarchy.
02
User Research Integration
Incorporated A/B testing data from prior campaigns to inform CTA placement, headline treatments, and where plan pricing needed to appear above the fold on mobile.
03
Module-by-Module XD Design
Designed each page module independently in Adobe XD — hero, plan cards, feature modules, cross-sell banners, FAQ — so each could be tested and iterated without full-page redesigns.
04
AEM Build & QA
Authored both pages in Adobe Experience Manager, working with front-end devs on component mapping and QA'd across Chrome, Safari, and Edge at all three breakpoints before launch.
Collaboration

Product, Marketing &
Engineering Alignment

Both product pages required alignment across three teams: marketing (promotional messaging), product (plan tier accuracy), and engineering (AEM component feasibility). I facilitated that alignment in weekly stakeholder reviews, presenting refined XD prototypes and incorporating feedback before each sprint closed.
