The Challenge

A Mark That Represents
an Entire Community

A school district logo is one of the most public-facing brand marks a community owns — appearing on everything from student backpacks to gymnasium walls, report cards to bus decals. The challenge was creating a mark that felt timeless rather than trendy, authoritative without being cold, and immediately recognizable at any size.

I developed multiple distinct logo concepts for Lexington Public Schools, each exploring different visual approaches to balance the district's academic heritage with a modern, scalable identity system. The concepts were delivered as fully vectorized AI and PDF files, ready for production across every application.

Design Process

Rigorous Approach to
Public Identity Design

01
Discovery & Research
Reviewed existing district materials, gathered stakeholder input on values and community pride, and audited peer school district identities to identify opportunities for differentiation.
02
Concept Development
Sketched and refined multiple logo directions in Adobe Illustrator — exploring lettermarks, wordmarks, icon+wordmark combinations, and mascot-integrated approaches across a range of visual tones.
03
Application Testing
Tested every concept across real use cases: 1-inch business card, 12-foot gym banner, grayscale photocopy, website favicon, and embroidery simulation — identifying which concepts remained legible across all scenarios.
04
Final Deliverables
Packaged final concepts as fully layered Adobe Illustrator files plus PDF presentations for stakeholder review — including color variations (full color, single color, reversed, grayscale) and recommended usage guidelines.
